Causal-HRI: Causal Learning for Human-Robot Interaction

Workshop at ACM/IEEE International Conference on Human-Robot Interaction 2024

March 11, 2024 US Mountain Time

Location: TBD/Hybrid

About The Workshop

For real-world robotic applications, it is imperative that robots can perceive and understand their surroundings, in order to learn to operate in human-centred environments. Over the past few decades, we have witnessed remarkable advancements in this domain, with robots now equipped with exceptional sensing and perception capabilities. However, much of this progress is grounded in machine learning (ML) techniques focused on pattern recognition and statistical correlation, often neglecting the exploration of the intrinsic structures, interdependencies between variables in observational data, dynamic aspects, and the underlying causal relationships that govern the emergence of these dependencies. Causality, on the other hand, specifically seeks to understand the causal structure or relationship within the data. It has been posited that many of the existing ML and Human-Robot Interaction (HRI) challenges, e.g. non-generalisability and bias, can be attributed to not taking into account the cause-and-effect relationships between the different data variables. Thus, we believe there is a need to bring together research perspectives from Causal Inference and Causal Learning to Human-Robot Interaction in order to embed robots with capabilities to discover and infer cause-and-effect relationships from its observations and to allow robots to generalise better to related, albeit, unseen settings. For the proposed half-day workshop, we intend to invite up to three keynote speakers from a mix of the aforementioned backgrounds (Causal Discovery and Inference, Causal Representation Learning, Robotics and Human-Robot Interaction) to share their perspectives on how such a goal can be achieved. Furthermore, we will invite 3-4 pages (excluding references/appendices) of contributions in order to further research in this field.
